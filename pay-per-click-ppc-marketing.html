PPC Benefits

Pay-per-click marketing allows your company to advertise in a fast, cost-effective and highly targeted manner on the internet to generate quality leads. Whereas SEO is generally a long-term lead generation strategy, PPC allows even recently launched websites to acquire new leads from potential clients.

1. Fast Results

You can get up and running quickly with PPC. Compared with SEO which can take several months for tangible results, with PPC the results are instant within minutes of launching your campaign.

PPC allows you to quickly cast a wide net to help you find new leads and customers.

Compared to other methods such as email and social media marketing, PPC allows you to target people outside of those who are already aware of your brand and doesn't limit to you to existing followers or customers.

2. Highly Targeted

PPC allows you to target your potential clients based on specific criteria such as location, time, day of week and many other options.

In doing such, you get to filter potential clients to your criteria.

This includes both targeting keywords through text ads and running ads through remarketing based on past behaviours.

3. Under Your Control

While there are several nuances regarding campaign settings, PPC gives you ultimate control over a wide range of options on how you reach potential clients. This starts with keyword placement and how restrictive you want to be.

If you want to start small, you have a lot of budget flexibility. If you're seeing positive results, you can scale up immediately and if you want to take a break, you can pause your campaign immediately. This level of control is hard to do with other advertising channels.

4. Measurable

One of the biggest benefits of PPC advertising with Google AdWords is that it's easy to measure and track: simply link the AdWords tool together with Google Analytics.

You'll get all high-level performance statistics including impressions, clicks and conversions. It's easy to determine how your campaigns are performing and which keywords are driving your traffic, which isn't as obvious in other advertising channels.

5. Marketing Data

PPC gives you a wealth of marketing data beyond even whats available directly in Google AdWords. PPC data from impression, click and conversion can further refine a website's SEO strategy.

You can also integrate third-party tools to guage where your competition is to help you learn how much market share you can gain.
